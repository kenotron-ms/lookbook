Hospitality
as an Art Form.

We have spent sixty-three years refusing the word "amenity." A Maison Duval property has no amenities — it has a kitchen garden, a wine cellar, a library that was curated by a single critic over eleven years, and staff who have been with us longer than most of our guests have been alive.

Our rooms do not have tablets. They have reading lamps selected for their colour temperature. Our restaurants are not destinations. They are the reason guests sometimes forget to leave.
