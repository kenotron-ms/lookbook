The Archiv Philosophy

"We do not make furniture. We resolve the question of how a room should be inhabited."

— Henrik Maas, Founder & Designer

Designed to outlast everything around it.

Every Archiv object begins as a structural problem. The chair must support without strain. The table must anchor without dominating. What remains after every excess is removed—that is the finished piece.

We work exclusively with materials that age with dignity: solid hardwoods, hand-treated leathers, woven natural fibres. Nothing that fades poorly. Nothing that isn't worth inheriting.

How it's made

A deliberate
sequence of
four steps

01

Commission

Each order begins with a conversation. We understand the room, the light, the life it serves. Dimensions are confirmed. Materials selected from our current stock.

02

Material Selection

Timber is chosen by hand from our Lisbon yard. Each board is evaluated for grain, density, and character. We reject as much as we accept.

03

Construction

Joinery is traditional: mortise and tenon, hand-cut dovetails, no metal fixings where wood will do. Each joint is a structural decision.

04

Delivery & Placement

White-glove delivery to your door. Our team places and levels every piece. We don't leave until the room is right.
