AURA Reference

Our planar magnetic flagship. Walnut ear cups machined from a single block of American black walnut, paired with lambskin earpads. Open-back — not for commuting. For sitting still and listening properly.

Why planar magnetic?

Dynamic drivers move a cone. Planar drivers move an entire membrane — more surface area means more even force distribution, lower distortion at high volumes, and a more accurate transient response. The trade-off: they're harder to drive. You need an amplifier.

AURA Studio

Closed-back with active noise cancellation built around a beryllium dome driver. The anodized aluminium headband flexes without creaking. 38 hours battery. Folds flat. Won't embarrass you at an airport.

Why beryllium?

Beryllium is 7x more rigid than titanium at 1/3 the weight. A stiffer dome means less breakup distortion in the high frequencies. It's expensive to work with, which is why most companies use it only in £2,000+ speakers. We put it in an everyday headphone because the ears notice.

AURA Field

Hybrid driver true wireless. Knowles balanced armature for detail, dynamic driver for bass impact. Machined aluminium shells. aptX Adaptive for 24-bit lossless streaming when your source supports it.

What is aptX Adaptive?

Most wireless earphones cap at 256kbps (SBC) or 328kbps (AAC). aptX Adaptive scales from 276kbps to 420kbps and supports 24-bit, 96kHz audio. If your Android phone and streaming service support it, you're getting CD quality wirelessly. iPhone users get AAC — still excellent.
